What UrbanBrief does now

How the online PD (BR) service works

1) Scope & appointment

  • Confirm whether a named PD (BR) is required based on project team structure.
  • Agree boundaries: what’s included, excluded, and where specialist input is needed.
  • Issue appointment with proportionate duties, deliverables and communication plan.

2) Compliance leadership

  • Create / maintain a live Design Risk Register focused on Building Regulations risk.
  • Coordinate designers, client and contractor actions around compliance-critical decisions.
  • Track how risks are eliminated, reduced, or controlled — with evidence, not assumptions.

3) Outputs that stand up

  • Compliance strategy narrative and signposting (clear and readable, not fluff).
  • Information handovers as design develops and responsibilities change.
  • A tidy audit trail to reduce disputes and ambiguity later.

What we typically ask for

  • Existing plans / survey (if available)
  • Proposed drawings (any stage)
  • Scope of works / brief
  • Known constraints (fire, structure, drainage, access)
  • Project team list (designer(s), contractor(s), consultants)

What we don’t do (unless agreed)

  • Clerk of Works / site supervision
  • Warranties or certification of construction
  • Replace Building Control inspection or approval
  • Act as PD on HRB projects (special arrangements required)

Scope & limitations (read this)

  • UrbanBrief provides Principal Designer (Building Regulations) services on a proportionate basis to the project’s scale and complexity.
  • We do not act as Building Control, do not certify construction, and cannot guarantee approval or outcomes.
  • Where specialist design is required (e.g. fire engineering, complex structure), we will recommend appropriate consultant input.
  • Unless expressly agreed, our service is delivered digitally and does not include site inspections or clerk-of-works duties.
  • Higher-risk buildings (HRB) and regulated regimes may require separate scoping and specialist arrangements.
