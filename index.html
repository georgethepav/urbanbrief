Fast-track projects often fail at coordination. We keep Building Regs risk visible, evidenced, and properly managed through design changes and programme pressure.

Important: UrbanBrief is not Building Control and cannot “approve” works. We coordinate compliance activity under the dutyholder regime; approval remains with the Building Control Body.

Example — avoiding a bad outcome

Scenario: A domestic extension is designed with multiple parties (designer + engineer + contractor). No formal PD (BR) appointment is made. A late change triggers structural and fire-safety implications, causing redesign, delay, and dispute over who should have flagged the risk.

UrbanBrief approach: Appoint a named PD (BR), maintain a Design Risk Register, and coordinate compliance-critical decisions early so risks are identified, responsibilities are clear, and the audit trail is tidy.

How the online PD (BR) service works

1) Scope & appointment

  • Confirm whether a named PD (BR) is required for your project team structure.
  • Agree boundaries: what’s included, what’s excluded, and where specialist input is needed.
  • Appointment issued with proportionate duties, deliverables and communication plan.

2) Compliance leadership

  • Create / maintain a live Design Risk Register focused on Building Regulations risk.
  • Coordinate designers, contractor and client actions around compliance-critical decisions.
  • Track how risks are eliminated, reduced or controlled — with evidence, not assumptions.

3) Outputs that stand up

  • Clear compliance narrative and signposting (readable, proportionate, project-specific).
  • Information handovers as the design develops and responsibilities change.
  • A tidy audit trail to reduce disputes and ambiguity later.

What we typically ask for

  • Existing plans / survey (if available)
  • Proposed drawings (any stage)
  • Scope of works / brief
  • Known constraints (fire, structure, drainage, access)
  • Project team list (designer(s), contractor(s), consultants)

What we don’t do (unless agreed)

  • Clerk of Works / site supervision
  • Warranties or certification of construction
  • Replace Building Control inspection or approval
  • Act as PD on HRB projects (special arrangements required)
