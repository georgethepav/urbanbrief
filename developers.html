Example scenario

HMO that wasn’t viable – caught before purchase.

A classic pattern: a buyer sees a terraced house in a strong rental area and wants to convert it to an HMO. On paper the numbers look great – until local policy and Article 4 are checked properly.

What the buyer wanted

Prospective purchaser spots a 3-bed house on your books and wants to turn it into a 6-bed HMO. Quick back-of-the-envelope maths says it’s a great yield. They ask you:

  • “Is this definitely fine under permitted development?”
  • “Any issues with Article 4 or local HMO caps?”
  • “Can I go bigger if the council allows it?”
What UrbanBrief did

They booked a £135 Planning Reality Check through you. We:

  • Checked local policies and SPD on HMOs
  • Reviewed the council’s Article 4 directions and mapping
  • Looked at nearby approvals and refusals for similar schemes

Result: the property sat inside an Article 4 area with a high existing concentration of HMOs. Our view was that a new HMO was unlikely to be supported – and not PD.

The buyer didn’t proceed – but they avoided a bad purchase, and you avoided being blamed for “implying it would be fine”. Next time, the same buyer is more likely to come back to you with confidence.
